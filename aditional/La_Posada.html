La Posada de los Muertos is the eleventh single by Mägo de Oz and the first from the album Gaia II: La Voz Dormida.

It was written by Txus Di Fellatio. The theme is an invitation to live, that no matter what problems we may have, we have to get up, insist and learn.

Musically, this song has a very festive rhythm and a very catchy chorus. It contains whistle and violin arrangements, loaded with Celtic and Spanish folk influences; all this without losing the heavy bases in the guitars, drums and bass. The vocal line is quite simple and the guitar solo is performed by guitarist Carlitos, who uses a wah-wah effect at the beginning. At the end of the song there is an accordion arrangement and vocalist Jose Andrëa delivers his classic "Cabrooones" shout. On this, their ninth single Mägo de Oz continues to use Gaboni's drawing for the cover highlighting skulls inside, but contradicting the original idea of the videoclip to represent the dead with puppets.
